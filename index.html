About

I'm a second-year civil engineering student at COEP Technological University in Pune. Alongside coursework, I work with Shashank Mehendale & Associates, a project management consultancy, where I've spent the past year on a residential development in Mumbai — reading daily progress reports, tracing schedule variances, and learning why buildings take as long as they do. It turns out the answer is rarely concrete and steel. It's usually coordination, incentives, and paperwork.

That discovery has quietly redirected my curiosity. My questions have been drifting from how things get built to how they get decided and financed — and I'm still working out what to do about that. In the meantime I'm studying software engineering on the side, because most of the problems I've met so far were solved faster with a script than a spreadsheet.

Away from all of it: I'm building a quadcopter from parts, I model things in Fusion 360 that I'll probably never manufacture, I play squash badly but often, and I take my V60 pour-over more seriously than is reasonable.
